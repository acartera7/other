📝 Tips for Writing Your Own Decoder

When writing an MP3 parser/decoder, these are the basic steps most decoders use:

Find frame sync (11 or 12 consecutive 1 bits in the header).

Parse header fields (bitrate index, sample rate index, padding, channel mode, etc.).

Compute frame size from header parameters (bitrate, sample rate, padding).

Read side info and main data blocks.

Decode the compressed audio (subband and Huffman decoding, inverse quantization, etc.).

Output PCM samples at the correct sample rate.

If you combine the PDF/tutorials above with a small decoder like minimp3 as a reference implementation, you’ll have a very solid foundation to understand and build your own. Let me know if you want a step-by-step breakdown of MP3 frame decoding logic in code!
